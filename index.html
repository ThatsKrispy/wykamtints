Why ceramic

Not all tint is the same film.

Cheap dyed film blocks light but lets heat through, then turns purple and bubbles. Our ceramic does the actual job.

88%

Heat rejection

Nano-ceramic particles bounce infrared heat before it hits the glass, so the cabin stays cool without going pitch black.

99%

UV protection

Blocks the rays that crack dashboards, fade leather and age skin. Like SPF for everything behind the glass.

0%

Signal loss

Ceramic isn't metal, so it never interferes with GPS, cell, Bluetooth or toll transponders. Metallic tint can't say that.

Good to know

Questions, answered.

Florida law sets minimum visible-light-transmission levels that differ for front, side and rear windows. We tint to the darkest legal shade for your vehicle and walk you through the options before any film touches the glass.

Dyed film is the cheapest — it darkens windows but lets heat through and fades to purple. Carbon is colorfast and looks great. Ceramic adds serious infrared heat rejection without going metallic, so it blocks heat while keeping your signals clear. We don't install dyed film.

Most full-vehicle tints are done the same day, usually two to four hours. We'll give you a window when you book so you can plan around it.

Give it a few days to cure — about three to five depending on weather. A little haze or tiny water pockets right after install are normal and clear as it dries. Full care instructions come with every job.

Yes — and removal is free when you're getting new film from us. Peeling, bubbling or purple tint gets stripped, the glass is cleaned up, and fresh ceramic goes on.

Against bubbling, peeling, cracking, fading and discoloration for as long as you own the vehicle. It's registered in your name at install, so there's nothing for you to file away.
